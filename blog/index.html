A retrospecive of multilingual sentence encoders

This is a post with notes on the paper Multilingual Universal Sentence Encoder for Semantic Retrieval by Yinfei Yang, Daniel Cer et al.

Existing large language models such as BERT obtain state-of-the-art performance on many natural language understanding tasks. They are pretrained to create contextual word embeddings and sentence embeddings via masked language modelling and next sentence predictions. In monolingual settings they are very effective for both word-level and sentence level tasks. Multilingual representations of sentences were not as effective in cross-lingual settings.
